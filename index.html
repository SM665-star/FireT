Module 3 — Understanding Fire

THE FIRE TRIANGLE

Every fire needs three elements. Click each side of the triangle to understand what it means in your warehouse — then remove each element to extinguish the fire.

Heat
Ignition Source
▶

Heat is the energy source that starts a fire. It raises a fuel's temperature to its ignition point — the moment it begins to burn.

🏭 In Your Warehouse
Common Ignition Sources
Angle grinders / cutting tools producing sparks
Faulty or overloaded electrical equipment
Radiant or space heaters near combustible materials
Hot work (welding, grinding, cutting)
Engine heat from forklifts and vehicles
How to Remove Heat
Implement a hot work permit system
Maintain and PAT test all electrical equipment
Keep heaters clear of combustible materials
Apply cooling agent (e.g. water extinguisher on Class A fires)
📦
Fuel
Combustible Material
▶

Fuel is anything that will burn. In a warehouse environment, fuel is everywhere — from the goods you store to the packaging and chemicals you use daily.

🏭 In Your Warehouse
Common Fuel Sources
Cardboard boxes, pallets, and packaging materials
Flammable liquids, solvents and glues
Racking, wooden shelving, and timber
Waste materials left in corridors or bays
Clothing, paper, and foam packaging
How to Remove Fuel
Store flammable liquids and solvents in the designated Flam Steel Cabinet
Never store combustibles under stairs, in roof voids or along escape routes
Maintain a daily housekeeping rota — keep aisles clear of waste
Empty bins before close of shift
💨
Oxygen
Air Supply
▶

The main source of oxygen is simply the air around us. Fire needs at least 16% oxygen to burn (normal air contains ~21%). Some industrial premises have additional stored oxygen sources.

🏭 In Your Warehouse
Oxygen Sources to Manage
General air circulation through open doors, windows and vents
Ventilation, extraction, and air conditioning systems
Compressed oxygen cylinders (piped or stored)
Open loading bay doors increasing airflow
How to Remove Oxygen
Close fire doors — they are designed to starve fires of air
Store compressed oxygen cylinders in well-ventilated areas away from ignition sources
Use smothering agents (CO2 extinguisher, fire blanket) to cut off air supply
Switch off cylinder valves at end of working day
✅

FIRE EXTINGUISHED

You've successfully removed all three elements of the fire triangle. By controlling Heat, Fuel, and Oxygen, you prevent and extinguish fires in your workplace.
